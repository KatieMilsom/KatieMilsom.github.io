About Me

My name is Katie Milsom and I am a final year PhD student in the Astronomy department at the University of Exeter. During my PhD I also spent three months (February- April 2025) at Monash University in Melbourne.

I work with Tim Harries on observations and modelling of protoplanetary discs. I focus on shadows seen in scattered light observations and how they can be cast by warps (where the disc plane is not constant with radius).

My Research

Shadows are a common feature in scattered light observations (light scattered off the surface of the disc) of protoplanetary discs. Shadows can be cast due to a perturbation in the surface of the disc blocking the stellar light from reaching the outer parts of the disc. For example a misaligned region of the disc, or a warp (where the disc plane is not constant with radius).

I model the propagation of warps in protoplanetary discs using 1D warp propagtaion theory, and a fast radiative transfer code to produce models of scattered light images. This allows us to visualise the shadows cast by warps and further our understanding of the origins of shadows seen in observations.
